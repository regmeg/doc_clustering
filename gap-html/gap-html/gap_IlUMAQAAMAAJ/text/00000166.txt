over a country peopled by colonies of their own name

three times more extensive than that of their con­

quests. At a still greater distance the open towns of

Gaul were deserted, and the inhabitants of the for­

tified cities, who trusted to their strength and vigil­

ance, were obliged to content themselves with such

supplies of corn as they could raise on the vacant

land within the inclosure of their walls. The di­
