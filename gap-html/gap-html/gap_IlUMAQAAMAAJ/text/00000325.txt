At first he lurked in the villages of Bithynia, con­

By degrees he ventured into the capital, trusted his

life and fortune to the fidelity of two friends, a

senator and an eunuch, and conceived some hopes of

success, from the intelligence which he obtained of

the actual state of public affairs. The body of the

people was infected with a spirit of discontent: they

regretted the justice and the abilities of Sallust, who
