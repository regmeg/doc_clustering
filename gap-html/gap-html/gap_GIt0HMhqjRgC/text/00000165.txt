through the valleys of mount Atlas, from both the

Syrtes to the Atlantic ocean. A personal injury,

the unjust execution or murder of his brother, ren­

dered Antalus the enemy of the Romans. The defeat

of the Vandals had formerly signalized his valour;

the rudiments of justice and prudence were still more

conspicuous in a Moor; and while he laid Adrume-

tum in ashes, he calmly admonished the emperor that

the peace of Africa might be secured by the recal of

Solomon and his unworthy nephews. The exarch led
