wheat, was exchanged for seven pieces of gold; fifty

pieces were given for an ox, a rare and accidental

prize; the progress of famine enhanced this ex­

orbitant value, and the mercenaries were tempted to

deprive themselves of the allowance which was scarcely

sufficient for the support of life. A tasteless and

unwholesome mixture, in which the bran thrice ex­
