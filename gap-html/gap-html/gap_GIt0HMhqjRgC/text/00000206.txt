names of Marcellus and Sergius, the most virtuous

and the most profligate of the courtiers were asso­

ciated in the same designs. They had fixed the

time of the execution; their rank gave them access

to the royal banquet; and their black slaves were

stationed in the vestibule and porticos, to announce

the death of the tyrant, and to excite a sedition in

the capital. But the indiscretion of an accomplice

saved the poor remnant of the days of Justinian.

The conspirators were detected and seized, with

daggers hidden under their garments: Marcellus

died by his own hand, and Sergius was dragged from
