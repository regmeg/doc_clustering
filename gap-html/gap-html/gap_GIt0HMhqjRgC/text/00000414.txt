ber, on the approach of winter they retreated four­

score miles from the capital, to the isle of Cyzicus.

in which they had established their magazine of spoil

and provisions. So patient was their perseverance,

or so languid were their operations, that they re­

peated, in the six following summers, the same attack

and retreat, with a gradual abatement of hope and

vigour, till the mischances of shipwreck and disease,

of the sword and of fire, compelled them to relin­

quish the fruitless enterprise. They might bewail

the loss, or commemorate the martyrdom, of thirty

thousand Moslems, who fell in the siege of Constan­

tinople.
