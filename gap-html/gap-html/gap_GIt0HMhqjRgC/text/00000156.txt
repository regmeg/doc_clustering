compelled, by mutual lassitude, to consult the repose

of their declining age. At a conference held on the

frontier, each party, without expecting to gain credit,

displayed the power, the justice, and the pacific in­

tentions, of their respective sovereigns; but necessity

and interest dictated the treaty of peace, which was

concluded for a term of fifty years, diligently com­

posed in the Greek and Persian language, and at­

tested by the seals of twelve interpreters. The liberty

of commerce and religion was fixed and defined; the

allies of the emperor and the great king were included
