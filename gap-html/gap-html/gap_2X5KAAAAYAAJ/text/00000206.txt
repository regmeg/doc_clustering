XII. In this posture of affairs, fortune

seemed to open a flattering prospect. Otho

was master of the greatest part of Italy,

and his fleets had the command of the sea.

To the foot of the maritime Alps (a) the

country was in his possession. To pass

over those mountains, and make a descent

on Narbon Gaul, was the measure which

he had projected. To conduct that expe­

dition he appointed Suedius Clemens, An-

tonius Novellus, and iEmilius Pacencis.
