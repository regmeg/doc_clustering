They demanded his immediate execution.

But their views were too apparent. The

best and ablest men in Rome were doomed

to destruction by a set of men, who panted

to let loose their rage, and lay a scene of

blood, of plunder, and devastation. Otho

was not yet in fulness of power. His au­

thority was sufficient to command the per­

petration of crimes ; to prohibit them was

still beyond him. The part he assumed

was that of a man enraged, and bent on

some atrocious deed. In that pretended

fury, he ordered Celsus to be loaded with

irons, as a man reserved for heavier punish­

ment, and by that stratagem saved him

from destruction.
