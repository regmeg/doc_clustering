The minister was praised, and the private

man detested. The art of conciliating the

good will of others was his in an eminent

degree. With his inferiors he knew how

to soften authority; to his friends and

equals his address was courtly; and yet,

with these attractive arts, a man so va­

rious was fitter to raise others to the impe­

rial dignity, than to obtain it for himself.
